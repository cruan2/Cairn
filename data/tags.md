# Tag Ontology (controlled vocabulary)

Tags are the shared language between champion data and rules. Adding a champion means
tagging it from this list; adding a strategic concept means writing a rule that reads
these tags. Keep the list small and curated — an ontology that grows without discipline
stops being a join key.

## Win-condition / tempo
- `scaling` — gets stronger than average as the game goes; a reason to play for late.
- `earlygame` — front-loaded power; a reason to force action before ~20 min.
- `falloff` — actively *weak* late; the clock is their enemy.
- `hypercarry` — the whole team can be built around this champion late.

## Playstyle archetype
- `poke` — chips enemies down from range before committing.
- `siege` — takes towers/objectives with range and zone control.
- `pick` — kills an isolated target to create a numbers advantage.
- `catcher` — lands a single game-ending CC (hook/binding) that enables a pick.
- `teamfight` — wants 5v5; strong grouped.
- `wombo` — chains AoE CC/ults for a decisive teamfight swing.
- `splitpush` — pressures a side lane 1v1 instead of grouping.
- `dive` — jumps onto the back line / under tower.
- `allin` — commits to a single decisive trade/kill attempt.
- `roam` — leaves lane to make plays elsewhere.
- `kite` — deals damage while retreating; punishes over-commitment.

## Role in a fight
- `frontline` — soaks damage, stands in front.
- `peel` — protects a carry (part of "protect the carry").
- `enchanter` — buffs/heals/shields allies.
- `assassin` — bursts a single squishy target.
- `lanebully` — wins the early 1v1/2v2 lane by default.
- `objective` — strong at contesting dragon/baron (DPS to objectives).

## Fragility
- `squishy` — dies fast if caught; positioning-dependent.
- `immobile` — no reliable escape; vulnerable to engage/picks.

Every tag should answer a coaching question. If you can't say "a rule reads this to
tell the player something," don't add the tag.
